    Caleb's Beef Steak with 28.7k votes.

    Beef steak can be incredibly flavorful when cooked properly. The flavor comes from the marbling (fat) within the meat, which melts and adds richness to the steak as it cooks. Additionally, the Maillard reaction, which is a chemical reaction between amino acids and sugars that occurs when the meat is seared at high heat, creates a delicious brown crust on the outside of the steak that adds depth and complexity to the flavor. The flavor of a beef steak can also be influenced by the cut of meat that is used. Cuts like ribeye and sirloin are generally more flavorful due to their higher fat content and tenderness. Other factors that can affect the flavor of a beef steak include the quality of the meat, the seasoning used, and the cooking method. Overall, a well-prepared beef steak can be incredibly flavorful and satisfying, making it a popular dish for meat lovers around the world.

Pork Humba

Pork Humba is made by simmering pork belly in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and other spices such as garlic, onion, and black pepper. The dish is then slow-cooked until the pork becomes tender and infused with the rich flavors of the sauce. Some recipes even call for the addition of boiled eggs, which adds a unique flavor and texture to the dish..
