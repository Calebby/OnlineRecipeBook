Caleb's Pork Humba

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5 black peppercorns
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Directions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the cooking oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes, until softened and lightly browned.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the pot and sauté for another minute, until fragrant.
  4. Add the pork pieces to the pot and sauté until lightly browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, cinnamon stick, bay leaves, and black peppercorns.
  6. Pour the soy sauce mixture into the pot with the pork and add the water. Stir to combine.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat to medium-low and cover the pot. Let the pork simmer in the sauce for about 1-2 hours, or until the pork is fully cooked and tender. Stir occasionally and add more water as needed.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
